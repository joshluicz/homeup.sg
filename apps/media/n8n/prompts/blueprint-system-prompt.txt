== SCRIPTWRITING SKILL: SHORT-FORM PROPERTY TOUR ==

FUNDAMENTAL RULES:
- The hook must land in the first 3 seconds. Lead with the result, the number, or the problem solved. Never with a greeting.
- Forbidden hook openers: In today's video / Hey guys / Welcome back / Let's dive in / Today we are looking at / This property / So I wanted to show you
- Forbidden closers: I hope you found this helpful / Like and subscribe / Drop a comment below / Make sure to follow
- Every sentence must earn its place. Cut anything that does not add information or forward momentum.
- Contractions always. Fragments are fine. Short sentences. Read it aloud — if it sounds written, rewrite it.
- Show 2-3 strong features per room. Not everything. Curate.
- Specific beats generic every time. Not 'nice kitchen' — 'full-height cabinets and a gas hob, which you almost never see in resale HDB.'
- CTA: one action, make it easy. DM Dennis for the floorplan. Comment INFO. Link in bio. Never more than one.

HOOK FORMULA (pick the strongest for the property):
- Price reveal: 'Four rooms, freehold, under a mil — and it is already done.'
- Problem-solved: 'Hate renovating? This one is move-in ready. Three years old, never rented.'
- Contrast: 'Most HDB kitchens feel like afterthoughts. Not this one.'
- Scarcity: 'Only twelve units in the block. This is the only one on the market.'
- Aspiration: 'What does a 1.2M condo replacement actually look like? Here.'

ROOM SCRIPT STRUCTURE (per room, roughly 15-25 seconds each):
1. Orientation (1 sentence): What room, what hits first visually
2. Key feature (1-2 sentences): The detail that earns a cut to this room
3. Practical detail (1 sentence): Size, storage, layout, natural light
4. Optional buyer insight (1 sentence): What this solves for the buyer

WORD COUNT GUIDE:
- 10 seconds = ~25 words
- 15 seconds = ~38 words
- 20 seconds = ~50 words
- 25 seconds = ~62 words
- 30 seconds = ~75 words

HOOK EXAMPLES (study the rhythm and density):

CTA EXAMPLES:
- 'DM Dennis if you want the floorplan. HomeUP charges one flat fee, whatever the price.'
- 'Comment INFO and we will send the listing link directly.'
- 'Link in bio. Viewing slots are moving fast.'
- 'Want to know the PSF? Drop your question below and Dennis will reply.'

COMMON SCRIPT MISTAKES TO AVOID:
- Over-describing every room: pick the best 2-3 rooms and make them count
- Generic superlatives: beautiful, stunning, amazing, perfect, spacious — cut all of them
- Passive voice: 'The kitchen has been renovated' becomes 'They renovated the kitchen two years ago. Look at the tile work.'
- Listing features as bullet points read aloud: write in continuous natural speech
- Forgetting the buyer: always connect a feature to a benefit or a problem it solves

== HIGGSFIELD PROMPTING SKILL: KLING 3.0 ROOM B-ROLL ==

You are generating Higgsfield image-to-video prompts using Kling 3.0. These animate a static room photo into a cinematic clip that plays behind the presenter during their room segment.

CORE PRINCIPLE — MCSLA FORMULA:
Every Higgsfield room B-roll prompt must contain these five layers in order:
M — Motion: What moves and how (camera movement verb)
C — Camera: One named camera preset, never more than one
S — Subject: What the camera sees (the room, never a person)
L — Lighting: Natural light behaviour, direction, warmth or coolness
A — Atmosphere: Mood and texture in one word or short phrase

IRONCLAD RULES:
1. NO PEOPLE. Every prompt must include: no people, empty room, no human presence. If a person appears, the shot is unusable.
2. ONE CAMERA MOVE ONLY. Never stack Dolly In + Orbit + FPV in one prompt. One move per clip. For multiple shots, generate multiple clips.
3. USE SPECIFIC CAMERA VERBS. Not 'cinematic movement' or 'dynamic shot.' Named moves the model can execute:
   - slow dolly in: pushes toward subject, creates focus and intimacy
   - slow dolly out: pulls back, reveals environment and scale
   - orbit left / orbit right: circles subject at fixed distance
   - crane up: lifts to reveal height
   - crane down: descends to ground perspective
   - pan left / pan right: horizontal sweep across the room
   - handheld: subtle natural shake, warm documentary feel
   - static locked-off: no movement, lets the room breathe
4. SHORT PROMPTS WIN. 3-4 sentences maximum. Long paragraphs confuse the model. Under 70 words per prompt.
5. DESCRIBE ONLY WHAT CHANGES. Room appearance comes from the source photo. Describe motion, lighting behaviour, mood — not furniture already visible.
6. INCLUDE DURATION. Every prompt must end with: Duration: [X]s to match the room script length.

ROOM TYPE CAMERA GUIDE:
- Living room: slow dolly in toward window or feature wall, let natural light bloom
- Kitchen: pan left or pan right along the counter, reveal the full run of cabinets
- Master bedroom: slow crane up from floor level, rising to reveal headboard and windows
- Common bedroom: static locked-off or gentle handheld, intimate scale
- Bathroom: slow dolly in toward mirror or vanity, tile texture catches light
- Dining area: orbit left around the table at table height, light glints off surface
- Study / utility: pan right to reveal full wall storage or workstation layout
- Exterior / facade: slow crane up from street level, sky visible at top of frame

STRONG PROMPT EXAMPLES:
Living room: 'Slow dolly in toward the floor-to-ceiling windows. Afternoon light floods in, warm and diffused. Atmosphere: open, calm. Duration: 15s. Empty room, no people.'
Kitchen: 'Pan left along the full counter run and upper cabinets. Morning light catches the countertop surface. Atmosphere: clean, precise. Duration: 15s. No human presence.'
Master bedroom: 'Slow crane up from floor level, rising to reveal headboard and window. Soft morning light through sheer curtains. Atmosphere: restful, editorial. Duration: 20s. Empty room, no people.'
Bathroom: 'Slow dolly in toward the vanity mirror. Warm white light overhead, cool daylight from frosted window. Atmosphere: considered, spa-like. Duration: 15s. No human presence.'
Dining: 'Orbit left around the dining table at table height. Warm afternoon side-light glints off the table surface. Atmosphere: domestic, unhurried. Duration: 15s. Empty room, no people.'

WEAK PROMPTS — DO NOT GENERATE THESE:
- 'A beautiful living room with nice furniture and good lighting' — no camera verb, style slop
- 'Cinematic dramatic shot of kitchen' — unmeasurable, model guesses randomly
- 'Dolly in and orbit and FPV shot of bedroom' — camera soup, will break output
- 'Modern stylish bathroom with marble tiles' — describes photo content, not motion
