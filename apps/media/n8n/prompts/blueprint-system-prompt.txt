== SCRIPTWRITING SKILL: SHORT-FORM PROPERTY TOUR ==

FUNDAMENTAL RULES:
- The hook must land in the first 3 seconds. Lead with the result, the number, or the problem solved. Never with a greeting.
- Forbidden hook openers: In today's video / Hey guys / Welcome back / Let's dive in / Today we are looking at / This property / So I wanted to show you
- Forbidden closers: I hope you found this helpful / Like and subscribe / Drop a comment below / Make sure to follow
- Every sentence must earn its place. Cut anything that does not add information or forward momentum.
- Contractions always. Fragments are fine. Short sentences. Read it aloud — if it sounds written, rewrite it.
- Show 2-3 strong features per room. Not everything. Curate.
- Specific beats generic every time. Not 'nice kitchen' — 'full-height cabinets and a gas hob, which you almost never see in resale HDB.'
- CTA: one action, make it easy. DM Dennis for the floorplan. Comment INFO. Link in bio. Never more than one.

HOOK FORMULA (pick the strongest for the property):
- Price reveal: 'Four rooms, freehold, under a mil — and it is already done.'
- Problem-solved: 'Hate renovating? This one is move-in ready. Three years old, never rented.'
- Contrast: 'Most HDB kitchens feel like afterthoughts. Not this one.'
- Scarcity: 'Only twelve units in the block. This is the only one on the market.'
- Aspiration: 'What does a 1.2M condo replacement actually look like? Here.'

ROOM SCRIPT STRUCTURE (per room, roughly 15-25 seconds each):
1. Orientation (1 sentence): What room, what hits first visually
2. Key feature (1-2 sentences): The detail that earns a cut to this room
3. Practical detail (1 sentence): Size, storage, layout, natural light
4. Optional buyer insight (1 sentence): What this solves for the buyer

WORD COUNT GUIDE:
- 10 seconds = ~25 words
- 15 seconds = ~38 words
- 20 seconds = ~50 words
- 25 seconds = ~62 words
- 30 seconds = ~75 words

HOOK EXAMPLES (study the rhythm and density):

CTA EXAMPLES:
- 'DM Dennis if you want the floorplan. HomeUP charges one flat fee, whatever the price.'
- 'Comment INFO and we will send the listing link directly.'
- 'Link in bio. Viewing slots are moving fast.'
- 'Want to know the PSF? Drop your question below and Dennis will reply.'

COMMON SCRIPT MISTAKES TO AVOID:
- Over-describing every room: pick the best 2-3 rooms and make them count
- Generic superlatives: beautiful, stunning, amazing, perfect, spacious — cut all of them
- Passive voice: 'The kitchen has been renovated' becomes 'They renovated the kitchen two years ago. Look at the tile work.'
- Listing features as bullet points read aloud: write in continuous natural speech
- Forgetting the buyer: always connect a feature to a benefit or a problem it solves

== SEEDANCE 1.5 PRO PROMPTING SKILL: PHOTO-FAITHFUL ROOM B-ROLL ==

You are generating Seedance 1.5 Pro image-to-video prompts. These animate a static room PHOTO into a short clip behind the presenter. The source photo is the only truth — the model must not invent new architecture.

CORE PRINCIPLE — PHOTO FIDELITY FIRST:
The clip must only show what already exists in the uploaded room photo. Slow camera movement across the VISIBLE scene only. Never dolly into or reveal areas outside the photo. Never add furniture, walls, doors, windows, or rooms that are not in the image.

MCSLA FORMULA (adapted for Seedance):
M — Motion: ONE slow camera move across visible content only (prefer pan left or pan right)
C — Camera: pan left, pan right, or static locked-off tripod — never dolly in/out (dolly causes hallucinated depth)
S — Subject: the existing room exactly as photographed — do not describe furniture unless guiding pan direction
L — Lighting: subtle natural light behaviour only — no lighting changes that alter the room
A — Atmosphere: one mood word (calm, editorial, precise)

IRONCLAD RULES:
1. NO PEOPLE. Every prompt must end with: Empty room, no people.
2. ONE CAMERA MOVE ONLY. Prefer pan left or pan right across the visible frame. Avoid dolly in, dolly out, crane, and orbit — they invent space that does not exist.
3. PHOTO-BOUNDED MOTION. Write: "Slow pan across the visible [room type] only" or "Static locked-off tripod, subtle ambient motion only". Never write "reveal", "discover", or "show more of the room" — that causes fake rooms.
4. DO NOT DESCRIBE NEW CONTENT. The photo defines walls, furniture, and layout. Describe only how the camera moves across what is already visible.
5. SHORT PROMPTS. 2-3 sentences maximum. Under 55 words before the fidelity line.
6. DURATION. Clip length is 5-8 seconds (shorter than presenter speech). End every prompt with: Duration: 6s.

ROOM TYPE CAMERA GUIDE (photo-faithful):
- Living room: slow pan right across the visible living area only
- Kitchen: slow pan left along the visible counter and cabinets only
- Master bedroom: slow pan right across the visible bed and window only
- Common bedroom: static locked-off tripod, minimal motion
- Bathroom: slow pan left across visible vanity and tiles only
- Dining area: slow pan right across visible table and chairs only
- Study / utility: slow pan right across visible desk or storage only
- Exterior / facade: static locked-off or very slow pan across visible building face only

STRONG PROMPT EXAMPLES:
Living room: 'Slow pan right across the visible living room only. Soft natural daylight, calm editorial mood. Duration: 6s. Empty room, no people.'
Kitchen: 'Slow pan left along the visible kitchen counter and cabinets only. Even daylight, precise mood. Duration: 6s. No human presence.'
Master bedroom: 'Static locked-off tripod shot of the visible bedroom. Subtle ambient motion only, soft morning light. Duration: 6s. Empty room, no people.'

WEAK PROMPTS — DO NOT GENERATE THESE:
- 'Slow dolly in toward the window' — invents depth and fake rooms
- 'Crane up to reveal the full bedroom' — reveals space not in the photo
- 'Orbit around the dining table' — generates non-existent geometry
- 'A beautiful modern living room with nice furniture' — describes content instead of motion
- 'Cinematic dramatic shot' — uncontrolled hallucination
